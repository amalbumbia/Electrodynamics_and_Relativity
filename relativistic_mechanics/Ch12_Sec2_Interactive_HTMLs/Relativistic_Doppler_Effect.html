Doppler Shift with Accelerated Round Trip

An observer moving with constant proper acceleration perceives the frequency of light from a stationary source.
The observer accelerates away from the source, turns around at proper time \( \tau_{\text{turn}} \), and returns.

This is a demonstration of the relativistic Doppler effect. First, notice how there is a jump discontinuity at the turnaround time. This is merely due to the fact that we are arbitrarily putting this in the code (instantaneous decleration is unphysical). Nonetheless, notice the assymetry on each side. As we move away from the source we experience a redshift; however, as we move towards the source, we experience a very steep blueshift
